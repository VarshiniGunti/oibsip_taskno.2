Time line of Savitri bai

  • 1831 - Born in Naigaon, Maharashtra, India.
  • 1840s - Savitribai's husband, Jyotirao Phule, educated her, breaking societal norms of the time.
  • 1848 - Savitribai and Jyotirao Phule opened the first girls' school in Pune, Maharashtra. This was a significant step towards women's education in India.
  • 1852 - They established the Native Female School in Pune, which aimed to provide education to girls from all castes and backgrounds.
  • 1853 - Faced social opposition and harassment for her work in promoting education for women and lower-caste individuals.
  • 1863 - Founded the Satyashodhak Samaj, a social reform organization that worked towards equality for all, regardless of caste or gender.
  • 1873 - Efforts in the field of education continued, and she worked tirelessly to promote literacy among women.
  • 1897 - passed away on March 10, leaving behind a legacy of social reform and women's education in India.

"Her contributions played a crucial role in the social reform movements of her time and continue to inspire generations of activists and educators in India"
